WebTeX Nuclear Physics Fallback Test

This page tests the fallback system for nuclear physics notation that KaTeX struggles with. The extension should automatically detect these patterns and use the custom renderer.

Nuclear Physics Notation (KaTeX Problematic)

These expressions cause KaTeX parsing errors and should trigger the fallback to custom renderer.
Nuclear notation with text commands:
$\text{_Z^A X}$ - Basic nuclear notation
$\text{_88^226 Ra} \to \text{_86^222 Rn} + \text{_2^4 He}$ - Alpha decay
$\text{_6^14 C} \to \text{_7^14 N} + \text{e^-} + \bar{\nu}$ - Beta decay
Expected: These should render with subscripts and superscripts, not cause KaTeX errors.

Complex Nuclear Reactions

More complex nuclear physics expressions that should work with the custom renderer.
Nuclear reactions:
$\text{Z^A N} \to \text{{Z-2}^{A-4} N'} + \text{_2^4 He}$ - General alpha decay
$\text{Z^A N} \to \text{{Z+1}^{A} N'} + \text{e^-} + \bar{\nu}$ - Beta minus decay
$\text{Z^A N} \to \text{{Z-1}^{A} N'} + \text{e^+} + \nu$ - Beta plus decay
Specific examples:
$\text{_10^19 Ne} \to \text{_9^19 F} + \text{e^+} + \nu$ - Positron emission
$\text{Z^A N} + \text{e^-} \to \text{{Z-1}^{A} N'} + \nu$ - Electron capture
$\text{_Z^A N*} \to \text{_Z^A N} + \gamma$ - Gamma decay

Mixed Content Test

Testing nuclear physics content mixed with regular math that KaTeX can handle.
Mixed expressions:
The energy of a photon is $E = h\nu$ where $h$ is Planck's constant.
For nuclear decay: $\text{_Z^A N} \to \text{_Z^A N} + \gamma$ with energy $E = mc^2$
The decay constant is $\lambda = \frac{\ln(2)}{T_{1/2}}$ where $T_{1/2}$ is the half-life.
Expected: Regular math should render with KaTeX, nuclear notation with custom renderer.
